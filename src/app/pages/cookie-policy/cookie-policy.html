What cookies we use, why we use them, and how you can control them.

Last updated: March 1, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and understand how you use the site. Cookies do not contain viruses or malicious code.

2. Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Required?
Session Cookies Keep you logged in during your visit. Deleted when you close the browser. Essential
Authentication Cookies Remember your login state so you don't need to sign in on every page. Essential
Security Cookies Detect and prevent fraudulent activity and protect your account. Essential
Preference Cookies Remember your search preferences, language settings, and display choices. Functional
Analytics Cookies Help us understand which pages are visited most and how users navigate the platform. Optional

3. Third-Party Cookies

We use the following third-party services that may set their own cookies:

  • Payment Gateways: Our payment processors may set cookies for fraud detection during the payment process.
  • Analytics: We use privacy-focused analytics tools to measure platform performance. These do not track you across other websites.

We do not use advertising cookies or allow third-party advertisers to track you on our platform.

4. Local Storage

In addition to cookies, BusMate uses browser local storage to store your authentication token and session preferences. This is essential for the app to function and cannot be disabled while using the platform.

5. How to Control Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy → Cookies

Note: Disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in and using the booking features of BusMate.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy as our services evolve. Material changes will be communicated via a notice on the platform.
